# Selective Service Board (HMS-NFO Integration)

## Agency Overview

Manages draft registration to ensure preparedness for national defense, leveraging advanced data to maintain readiness.

**Core Function:** Emergency military personnel mobilization readiness

## First Principles Analysis

The SSB exists to maintain the infrastructure for rapidly mobilizing military personnel in a national emergency. Its core function is registering eligible individuals to enable fair and efficient draft implementation if authorized by Congress. The agency operates on the principle that national security preparedness requires maintaining systems for personnel mobilization even during peacetime. Its activities focus on registration compliance and database maintenance, recognizing that effective conscription readiness requires both current information on eligible individuals and established processes that could be quickly activated if needed.

### Fundamental Principles

- National defense requires capacity to rapidly expand military personnel
- Advance registration enables more orderly potential mobilization
- Fair selection systems distribute service obligations equitably
- Standby systems allow maintaining capabilities during peacetime

### Key Mechanisms

- Maintain database of potential conscripts for emergency use
- Establish protocols for selecting individuals in potential draft
- Provide mechanisms for conscientious objector processing
- Enable rapid scaling of military forces if authorized by Congress

## HMS-NFO Integration

The HMS-NFO system provides specialized data and analytics services tailored to Selective Service Board's mission. The following components are particularly relevant:

- **Internet Data Collection System**: Gathers trade and economic data from public sources
- **Data Access API**: Provides standardized access to HMS-NFO data
