# National Mediation Board (HMS-NFO Integration)

## Agency Overview

Facilitates labor-management relations in the railroad and airline industries, promoting peaceful dispute resolution.

**Core Function:** Transportation sector labor stability

## First Principles Analysis

The NMB exists to minimize disruptions in vital transportation services through specialized mediation of labor disputes. Its core function is facilitating agreements between labor and management in industries where work stoppages would have outsized economic impacts. The agency operates on the principle that transportation labor relations require distinct procedures due to their critical infrastructure role. Its activities emphasize extended mediation before release to self-help, recognizing that effective dispute resolution in these sectors requires both specialized industry knowledge and processes designed to exhaust negotiation possibilities before allowing strikes or lockouts.

### Fundamental Principles

- Rail and air transportation infrastructure constitutes critical economic arteries
- Transportation industry disruptions create widespread economic impacts
- Nation-wide transportation networks require sector-specific labor relations approaches
- Essential transportation services necessitate specialized dispute resolution

### Key Mechanisms

- Mediate collective bargaining disputes in rail and air industries
- Facilitate interest-based problem solving between parties
- Administer representation elections for transportation employees
- Provide arbitration services for grievance resolution

## HMS-NFO Integration

The HMS-NFO system provides specialized data and analytics services tailored to National Mediation Board's mission. The following components are particularly relevant:

- **Internet Data Collection System**: Gathers trade and economic data from public sources
- **Data Access API**: Provides standardized access to HMS-NFO data
